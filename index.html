Dear team at CUE,

I am applying for a student research assistant position at your chair. My interest in CUE's work stems from my ongoing engagement with the ecological and social dimensions of urban space — an interest that has shaped my studies, my activism, and my practice from the very beginning.

Over the course of my studies, it became increasingly clear to me that architecture is not only a design discipline, but a tool for making social structures visible and helping to reshape them. For several semesters I have been involved in the student collective perspektiv;wechsel, which I co-founded. Our lecture series and events engage critically with intersectional feminist questions of urban perception, reception, and power relations in the built environment. This work has strongly shaped my thinking and my design practice — and shown me how productive collective processes can be for architectural discourse and practice.

I do not see design, organisation, and politics as separate fields, but as mutually entangled levels of responsibility. In my current position as a working student at ZRS Architekten, I am involved in construction documentation and have developed a strong interest in questions of material-appropriate construction, building surveys, and damage mapping. Alongside this, I am working in my Master's programme on cartographic and spatio-temporal methods for analysing urban ecosystems — most recently through a transect of the Panketalniederung.

Projects such as the Böden Berlins seminar (SoSe 2025) show that spatio-temporal soil mapping is established as a critical practice at TU Berlin. My transect through the Panketalniederung connects to these questions — focusing on hydrological history, land ownership, and ecological habitability.

Beyond feminist urbanism, sustainability is a topic I continuously engage with. The construction sector bears significant responsibility for anthropogenic climate change and is urgently in need of reform. In the future, I want to dedicate myself to exactly this task and work towards more sustainable cities.

I look forward very much to the possibility of becoming part of the discourse at CUE.

Sincerely,
Lara Luise Brenner

with Eliott Croubalian  ·  SS 2026  ·  Semester project, ongoing

The transect was developed as a spatio-temporal mapping instrument that makes geological strata, land ownership patterns, vegetation history, and hydrological traces simultaneously legible. It traverses the Panketalniederung and the Barnim plateau, operating with historical maps from 1888 to 2025.
